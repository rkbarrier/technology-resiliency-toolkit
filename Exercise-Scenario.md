# Technology Resiliency Exercise Scenario

## Executive Overview

This document presents a sample tabletop exercise designed to evaluate organizational readiness during a technology disruption. The objective is to validate recovery procedures, improve coordination between business and technology teams, and identify opportunities to strengthen operational resilience.

> **Portfolio Note:** This is an original example created for professional portfolio purposes. It demonstrates technology resiliency exercise planning concepts and does not contain confidential or proprietary information from any employer.

---

# Exercise Objectives

The exercise is designed to:

* Validate Business Continuity Plans.
* Validate Disaster Recovery procedures.
* Evaluate incident response coordination.
* Confirm stakeholder communication processes.
* Identify gaps in recovery planning.
* Improve organizational readiness.

---

# Scenario Overview

At 9:15 AM on a weekday, the organization's primary data center experiences a significant infrastructure failure following a power distribution event.

Several customer-facing applications become unavailable, including:

* Online Banking
* Payment Processing
* Customer Authentication
* Treasury Operations

Initial estimates indicate that the outage may exceed established Recovery Time Objectives (RTOs).

Executive leadership activates the Technology Resiliency Program and initiates disaster recovery procedures.

---

# Participants

* Executive Leadership
* Technology Recovery Team
* Business Continuity Team
* Information Security
* Operations
* Application Owners
* Communications Team
* Risk Management

---

# Discussion Questions

## Phase 1 – Incident Response

* How is the incident identified and escalated?
* Who declares a disaster?
* What information is communicated to leadership?

---

## Phase 2 – Recovery

* Which applications are restored first?
* How are recovery priorities determined?
* What dependencies could delay recovery?

---

## Phase 3 – Business Operations

* Which manual workarounds are available?
* How are customers informed?
* How is operational risk monitored?

---

## Phase 4 – Executive Decision Making

* When should alternate recovery strategies be activated?
* What metrics indicate recovery success?
* What executive decisions require formal approval?

---

# Exercise Success Criteria

The exercise is considered successful if participants:

* Follow documented recovery procedures.
* Demonstrate clear communication.
* Restore critical services within recovery objectives.
* Identify improvement opportunities.
* Document action items and ownership.

---

# Post-Exercise Activities

* Conduct a lessons learned meeting.
* Update recovery documentation.
* Revise Business Continuity Plans as needed.
* Update Disaster Recovery procedures.
* Assign owners and target dates for improvement actions.
