# Recovery Strategy

## Executive Overview

This Recovery Strategy outlines the approach for restoring critical technology services following a disruptive event. It establishes recovery priorities, identifies key dependencies, and defines the coordination required between business and technology teams to resume operations safely and efficiently.

> **Portfolio Note:** This document is an original example created for professional portfolio purposes. It demonstrates technology resiliency planning concepts and does not contain confidential or proprietary information from any employer.

---

# Recovery Objectives

The primary objectives of the recovery strategy are to:

* Restore critical business services within established Recovery Time Objectives (RTOs).
* Minimize operational disruption.
* Protect customer data and information assets.
* Maintain regulatory and policy compliance.
* Ensure business operations resume in a controlled and validated manner.

---

# Recovery Approach

Recovery activities are prioritized according to business criticality.

## Phase 1 – Stabilization

* Confirm incident scope.
* Activate the recovery team.
* Notify executive leadership.
* Assess technology impacts.
* Establish communication channels.

---

## Phase 2 – Infrastructure Recovery

* Restore network connectivity.
* Recover compute and storage resources.
* Validate database availability.
* Verify identity and access services.

---

## Phase 3 – Application Recovery

Applications are restored based on business priority.

Priority order:

1. Customer-facing platforms
2. Payment processing systems
3. Treasury operations
4. Reporting platforms
5. Internal support applications

---

## Phase 4 – Business Validation

Business representatives validate:

* Application functionality
* Data integrity
* End-to-end transaction processing
* User access
* Operational readiness

# Recovery Dependencies

## Technology

* Network infrastructure
* Identity management
* Database platforms
* Cloud services
* Monitoring tools

## Business

* Application owners
* Operations teams
* Vendor support
* Executive leadership
* Risk management

---

# Success Criteria

Recovery is considered successful when:

* Critical applications are operational.
* Business owners approve production readiness.
* Customer services are available.
* No material data loss has occurred.
* Monitoring confirms stable system performance.

---

# Risks

| Risk                   | Mitigation                    |
| ---------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Infrastructure failure | Redundant architecture        |
| Vendor outage          | Alternate recovery procedures |
| Data corruption        | Backup validation             |
| Resource availability  | Cross-trained recovery teams  |
| Communication delays   | Predefined communication plan |

---

# Continuous Improvement

Following each recovery event or exercise:

* Conduct a lessons learned session.
* Update recovery documentation.
* Review recovery metrics.
* Revise recovery priorities if needed.
* Incorporate improvements into future exercises.
